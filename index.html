About the Summit

The Global Education Summit aims to raise awareness about the plight of out-of-school children and street children, and to propose sustainable solutions to ensure access to quality education for all.

The summit will bring together dignitaries, policymakers, educators, and stakeholders from around the world to share experiences, best practices, and innovative solutions.

Consensus Statement

A joint declaration outlining key actions to address out-of-school children.

Solution Framework

Policy recommendations and innovative solutions to achieve education for all.

Partnerships

New collaborations to support education initiatives for out-of-school children.

H.E. Karisa Nzai Menza

Political Affairs Advisor, Executive Office of the President of Kenya

H.E. Karisa Nzai Menza is a Kenyan public official who currently serves as the Political Affairs Advisor in the Executive Office of the President of Kenya, H.E. William Ruto.

As the Political Affairs Advisor, he plays a key role in advising the President on political matters and promoting the government's agenda, particularly in the Coast region of Kenya.

Key Roles & Responsibilities

  • Political Affairs Advisor to the President of Kenya
  • Active political figure in the Coast region of Kenya
  • Engages with local leaders and community groups
  • Advocates for regional support of national government
  • Former aspirant for Jomvu constituency MP seat

Key Statements & Advocacy

  • Urges Coast leaders to work with the Kenya Kwanza government
  • Advocates for unity among Kenyans
  • Promotes vigilance against issues like cyberbullying
  • Focuses on ensuring the Coast region benefits from national development

The honorific "H.E." (His Excellency) is often used in the context of his high-ranking advisory role within the Executive Office of the President.

Baroness Sandip Verma

Member of House of Lords, Chancellor of University of Roehampton

Baroness Sandip Verma is a British Indian politician, businesswoman, and life peer who currently sits as a Conservative member in the House of Lords.

Born in Amritsar, Punjab, India, in 1959, she moved to the UK as a child. She is an advocate for equality, diversity, and inclusion and has received several awards, including one from the President of India in 2011 for her contributions to public affairs and promoting the interests of overseas Indians.

Current Roles

  • Member of the House of Lords (since 2006)
  • Chancellor of the University of Roehampton (since 2022)
  • Chair of the UN Women's National Committee UK
  • Ministerial Champion for Tackling Violence Against Women and Girls Overseas (since 2019)
  • Businesswoman with directorships in private sector

Past Government Service

  • Parliamentary Under-Secretary of State for International Development (2015–2016)
  • Parliamentary Under-Secretary of State for Energy and Climate Change (2012–2015)
  • Government Whip/Baroness-in-waiting (2010–2012)

She started her first business at age 19 in high fashion and later moved into the service sector. She holds directorships and advisory roles in the private sector, focusing on areas like ESG funds and green projects.
